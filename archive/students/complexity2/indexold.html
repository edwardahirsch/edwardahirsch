Literature:

  1. For transparent proofs (question 1), I strongly recommend your own notes. If there are none, you may use the following paper

    S. Hougardy, H. J. Prömel, A. Steger, Probabilistically Checkable Proofs and Their Consequences for Approximation Algorithms, Discrete Mathematics 136 (1994), 175-223 ; reprinted in: Trends in Discrete Mathematics (W. Deuber, H.J. Prömel, B. Voigt, eds.), North Holland, 1995.

    You need mainly pages 3, 4, 14 and 19-23.

  2. There is a very good (and very large) survey on cryptography (caution: it contains a lot of typos):

    Shafi Goldwasser and Mihir Bellare, Lecture Notes on Cryptography (MIT, 1996-99).

    Use Sections 3 and 9 (also Sections 2 and 7 may be helpful for those who missed the first lecture on cryptography (RSA and one-way functions)). This course covers questions 3, 5, 6, 7, and may cover more.

  3. Shor's factorization algorithm (question 9) is published in the paper

    Peter Shor, Polynomial-time algorithms for prime factorization and discrete logarithms on a quantum computer, SIAM Journal of Computing 26, pp. 1484-1509 (1997).

  4. You may improve your computational complexity background by reading

    Oded Goldreich, Introduction to Complexity Theory (Lecture Notes, Weizmann Institute of Science, 1998-99).

  5. Another source:

    Christos H. Papadimitriou, Computational Complexity, Addison-Wesley, 1994.

    Computational complexity background + RSA "in simple words" (question 2) + question 4.

  6. Yet another source:

    Kitaev, Shen, Vyalyi (IN RUSSIAN), Klassicheskie i kvantovye vychislenija.

    This book is probably still available in "Dom Knigi" etc. It contains some background on computational complexity in general and a COMPLICATED stuff about quantum algorithms. I recommend that you read it only to refresh Grover's algorithm (question 8), or for general computational complexity background.
